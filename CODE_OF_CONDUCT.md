# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Mira to be a project anyone can contribute to without fear of being treated badly because of who they are. We commit to making participation in this project a respectful and constructive experience.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful in disagreements. Critique ideas, not people.
- Assume good faith in others. Ask questions before assuming intent.
- Welcome new contributors. The first PR is hard for everyone.
- Keep technical discussion technical.
- Take feedback gracefully.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, harassment, or insults — public or private.
- Demeaning, discriminatory, or derogatory language.
- Publishing other people's private information without consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or reviews.
- Any other conduct a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces: the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, the GitHub Sponsors page, and any community channels we run. It also applies when an individual is representing the project externally.

## Reporting

Report concerns privately to **support@miracode.ai**. Reports are confidential. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe and take action proportionate to the situation, which may include warnings, temporary bans, or permanent bans.

Maintainers who fail to enforce this policy in good faith may face the same consequences.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by the spirit of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), simplified for clarity.
